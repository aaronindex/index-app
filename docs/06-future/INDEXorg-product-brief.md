# INDEXorg — Product Brief

## Overview

INDEXorg extends INDEX from an individual thinking ledger into a shared structural ledger for teams.

Where INDEX captures and distills thinking for one person, INDEXorg allows teams to:

- preserve decisions
- surface unresolved work
- maintain direction across time
- reduce context loss across people

INDEXorg does not replace existing tools like Slack, Docs, Jira, or AI assistants.

Instead, it sits **after thinking**, acting as the place where important parts of thinking are distilled into durable structure.

---

# The Core Problem

Modern teams produce enormous amounts of thinking across many surfaces:

- AI chats
- Slack threads
- docs
- meetings
- tickets
- email

This thinking is valuable, but it quickly fragments.

Typical outcomes:

- decisions get buried in chat
- teams rehash the same discussions
- new collaborators lack context
- projects drift
- documentation becomes stale

Most tools try to solve this by storing everything.

INDEXorg takes the opposite approach.

Instead of saving everything, it extracts **important parts**.

---

# Product Philosophy

INDEXorg follows the same philosophy as INDEX:

- thinking happens elsewhere
- INDEX captures moments of thinking
- reduction distills structure
- structure accumulates over time

INDEXorg does not generate ideas.

It reflects the structure that emerges from real work.

---

# Core Primitives

## Sources

A **Source** represents a moment of thinking.

Examples:

- an AI conversation
- a Slack thread
- meeting transcript
- doc excerpt
- ticket discussion
- email chain

Sources are inert until reduced.

---

## Reduction

Reduction extracts the signals that matter.

Signals include:

- Decisions
- Tasks
- Highlights
- Open loops (unresolved questions)
- Risks or assumptions (optional depending on team needs)

Reduction is the key moment where noise becomes structure.

---

## Structure

As reductions accumulate, structure emerges automatically.

INDEXorg surfaces patterns like:

- repeated decisions forming direction
- unresolved loops indicating tension
- task clusters indicating momentum
- structural shifts over time

Structure is never manually curated.

It emerges from reductions.

# Key Surfaces

INDEXorg introduces several surfaces beyond the individual INDEX product.

---

## Team Snapshot

A structural view of current team state.

Shows:

- active decisions
- unresolved loops
- current tasks
- recent structural shifts

Snapshot answers:

> What is happening right now?

---

## Direction

A higher-level structural report.

Shows:

- major decisions shaping direction
- emerging arcs across projects
- structural shifts over time

Direction answers:

> Where is this work going?

---

## Timeline

A structural timeline showing change over time.

Instead of showing activity volume, it shows:

- when reductions changed structure
- spacing between structural shifts

This allows teams to see:

- acceleration
- drift
- stagnation
- inflection points

---

## Ask INDEX

A structural query surface.

Example queries:

- "What decisions shaped this project?"
- "What open loops still exist?"
- "What changed in the last two weeks?"
- "Why did we pivot this direction?"

Ask INDEX answers using reduced signals, not raw chat history.

# Ideal Customers

INDEXorg is not for every team.

It works best where thinking is heavy and context matters.

Good fits:

- AI-native teams
- product teams
- research teams
- startup leadership teams
- strategy groups
- design orgs

Less relevant for:

- purely operational teams
- transactional workflows
- teams already tightly constrained by rigid process systems

---

# Why Now

AI has dramatically accelerated idea generation.

The new bottleneck is:

- decision continuity
- structural clarity
- team memory that actually works

INDEXorg addresses the gap between thinking and execution.

---

# Success Criteria

If INDEXorg works, teams should experience:

- fewer context-reset meetings
- clearer project direction
- faster onboarding into active work
- reduced context loss
- more coherent decision trails

The goal is not more documentation.

The goal is **less confusion**.
