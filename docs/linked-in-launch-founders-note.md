Most of my thinking now happens in conversations with AI.

A question leads to an idea.
An idea leads to a thread.
A thread leads to five more.

By the end of the day there are dozens of conversations across ChatGPT, Claude, and Cursor. Some contain real breakthroughs. Most are fragments of thinking that felt important in the moment.

The problem isn’t generating ideas.

The problem is remembering what actually mattered.

AI makes it incredibly easy to think.
It also makes it incredibly easy to lose the thread.

So I built INDEX.

Most tools help you generate ideas.
INDEX helps you see where those ideas are going.

You capture moments of thinking and reduce them to the signals that carry weight — decisions, results, and highlights.

Over time those signals accumulate and structure begins to appear.

Direction emerges.
Shifts mark meaningful change.
A timeline reveals the tempo of thinking over time.

INDEX isn’t another place to think.

It’s a ledger for thinking.

If you find yourself thinking across many AI conversations and wondering where it’s all going, INDEX was built for you.
