# Weekly Digest — Product Specification

## Purpose

Weekly Digest is a reflection artifact generated from the structural signals recorded in INDEX during a given week.

It answers a simple question:

> What happened in my thinking this week?

Unlike Direction, Shifts, or Timeline, the Digest is allowed to include light narrative language.

However, it must remain observational and grounded in recorded signals.

The Digest should never speculate, coach, or interpret motives.

Tone should feel like a **logbook entry** or **field note**.

---

## Information Sources

Digest should summarize signals from the selected week, including:

* decisions recorded
* results recorded
* structural shifts
* active arcs
* timeline pulses

---

## Output Structure

A digest contains four sections:

### Week Overview

A short paragraph summarizing the general focus of the week.

Example:

This week focused on stabilizing the semantic labeling system and clarifying the Direction model for INDEX v2.

### Structural Changes

List notable structural shifts recorded during the week.

Example:

Structural shifts recorded:

* Semantic overlay live
* v2 alpha readiness defined
* Structure updated

### Decisions and Results

Summarize decisions and results recorded during the week.

Example:

Decisions recorded: 2
Results recorded: 1

### Open Tension

List unresolved decisions or tensions that remain active.

Example:

Open decisions:

* Shift in INDEX's Purpose
* Homepage title direction

---

## Tone Rules

The digest should:

* remain factual
* avoid advice
* avoid speculation
* avoid interpretation of intent

The digest should read like a **weekly logbook entry for thinking**.

---

Use the following inputs:

Tone:
calm, observational, factual.

Do not speculate.
Do not give advice.
Do not interpret motives.

Structure the output using the four sections:

1. Week Overview
2. Structural Changes
3. Decisions and Results
